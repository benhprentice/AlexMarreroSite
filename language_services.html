Translations vs Adaptations

It’s important to make a clear distinction between a basic translation and a language/cultural adaptation. While I do both, I specialize in the latter. What does that mean?!

Is it telling the story in a relatable way?

Is it as clear, compelling and concise as it is in the original language?

Does it cross the nuanced cultural boundaries accurately?

Does it fit into an allotted :30 :15 or :06 time frame?

Most of the time, they do not.

Usually, a translation going from English to Spanish will require 3x the amount of words that are being used in English. It’s just the nature of the Spanish language. How do we tighten that up and keep the message accurate AND relatable? That’s the key.

That's storytelling.

An adaptation (or a transadaptation) requires creativity, copywriting & storytelling skills. A great adaptation captures the essence of the message/story in the original language and transforms it into the clearest and most relatable version of itself into the new language. It needs to accomplish that while simultaneously maintaining the original tone, essence & intent. All while considering not only a different set of grammatical language rules but also subtle cultural nuances & parameters.
