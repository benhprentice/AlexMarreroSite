Translations vs Adaptations

It’s important to make a clear distinction between a basic translation and a language/cultural adaptation. While I do both, I specialize in the latter. What does that mean?!

Is it telling the story in a relatable way?

Is it as clear, compelling and concise as it is in the original language?

Does it cross the nuanced cultural boundaries accurately?

Does it fit into an allotted :30 :15 or :06 time frame?

Most of the time a basic translation does not.

Usually, going from English to Spanish will require 3x the amount of words that are used in English. It’s just the nature of the Spanish language. How do we keep the message accurate, tight and relatable?

That's storytelling.

A successful adaptation (or a transadaptation) requires creativity, copywriting & storytelling skills. Not to mention a strong command of both languages and their inherent cultural differences. The adaptation must also maintain the integrity of the original tone and message, in what is usually a very short amount of time.
